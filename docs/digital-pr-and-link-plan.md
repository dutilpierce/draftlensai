# Digital PR & link acquisition plan

DraftLens ships **link-worthy assets** without fabricated data: methodology transparency, honest comparisons, and academy guides grounded in real workflow constraints.

## On-site assets (ready after deploy)

| Asset | Outreach angle |
|-------|----------------|
| `/research/benchmark-methodology` | “We publish how we would score document review systems — tasks, rubrics, disclosure — before claiming winners.” |
| `/research/ai-document-review-benchmark` | Framework-only page: invite collaborators to co-define tasks; no numeric vendor rankings until runs complete. |
| `/compare/draftlens-vs-*` | Pitch **workflow fit** stories to editors covering legaltech, AI writing, and enterprise productivity — not smear pieces. |
| `/academy/*` | Contributor-style tips for paralegal newsletters, law-school clinics, and technical writing communities. |
| `/methodology` + `/editorial-policy` | Trust pack for security reviews, procurement, and “how do you handle partial model agreement?” |

## Outreach angles (examples)

1. **Benchmark-driven:** offer to share rubrics with academic or industry working groups; ask for feedback, not backlinks as the opening move.
2. **Comparison-driven:** propose a neutral “how teams evaluate AI review tools” guest outline citing your comparison structure.
3. **Template / checklist:** redline / long-doc proofreading checklists derived from academy pages (PDF one-pager later — not required day one).

## Communities & publication types (targets, not endorsements)

- Legaltech newsletters, law blogs with tool roundups, ABA-adjacent technology sections (editorial discretion applies).
- Productivity / knowledge-work Substacks, Slacks, and Discords with **writing + compliance** audiences.
- AI tooling directories that accept **neutral** listings (avoid paid “top 10” spam farms).

## Before outreach begins

- [ ] Live site with stable `NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_URL`.
- [ ] `/contact` monitored.
- [ ] Methodology + benchmark framework pages live (no fake scores).
- [ ] One polished **product** screenshot set in `public/` (when available).

## What not to do

- Purchased links, PBNs, or “parasite SEO” on unrelated high-DA properties.
- Mass-generated city / industry doorway pages.
- Fake testimonials, logos, awards, or benchmark tables.
- FAQPage schema stuffed with questions users never see on-page.
- Harassing journalists or demanding follow links.
